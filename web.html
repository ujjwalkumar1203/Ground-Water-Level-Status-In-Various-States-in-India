India has a vast groundwater resource with an estimated annual recharge of 433 billion cubic meters (BCM). However, the groundwater level has been declining at an average rate of 0.3 meters per year. According to the Central Ground Water Board (CGWB), around 1,07,819 villages in India are currently facing groundwater depletion, and more than 60% of the wells are showing a declining trend in groundwater levels.

Jammu and Kashmir

Groundwater level status in Jammu and Kashmir : Stable

A study commissioned by the central government revealed that the underground water resources of Jammu and Kashmir are “safe” with less than 70% groundwater extraction, reported Press Trust of India (PTI).

Arunachal Pradesh

Groundwater level status in Arunachal Pradesh : Critical

the average groundwater level in Arunachal Pradesh was recorded at 2.74 meters below ground level (mbgl) during the post-monsoon season (January 2021), which is considered to be within the safe category. During the pre-monsoon season (March 2021), the average groundwater level was recorded at 3.05 mbgl, which is also considered to be within the safe category.

Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Groundwater level status in Andaman and Nicobar Islands : Stable

Nicobars is the top region by ground water level in India. As of 2019, ground water level in Nicobars was 3.91 meters below ground level that accounts for 50.59% of India's ground water level. The top 3 regions (others are South Andaman, and North & Middle Andaman) account for 100.00% of it. India's total ground water level was estimated at 7.73 meters below ground level in 2019.

MAHARASTRA

Groundwater level status in MAHARASTRA : Declining

As per the CGWB report, in pre-monsoon 2021, the average groundwater level in Maharashtra was 6.28 meters below ground level (mbgl), which is a decline of 0.21 mbgl compared to the average pre-monsoon level of the previous year. The groundwater level in the state varies widely across different districts and regions.

BIHAR

Groundwater level status in BIHAR : Critical

The groundwater level in Bihar earlier ranged from 40 feet to 200 feet, but the recent dips have brought down the range to between 60 and 250 feet.

West Bengal

Groundwater level status in West Bengal : Stable

As of August 2021, the average groundwater level in West Bengal was around 4.70 meters below ground level (MBGL), which is about 0.98 meters higher than the groundwater level in August 2020.

Gujarat

Groundwater level status in Gujarat : Stable

Gujarat ground water level was at level of 11.21 meters below ground level in 2019, down from 12.69 meters below ground level previous year, this is a change of 11.65%.

Madhya Pradesh

Groundwater level status in Madhya Pradesh : Stable

As of August 2021, the average groundwater level in Madhya Pradesh was around 13.02 meters below ground level (MBGL), which is about 0.29 meters lower than the groundwater level in August 2020.

Tamil Nadu

Groundwater level status in Tamil Nadu : Declining

As of 2021, the average groundwater level in Tamil Nadu was reported to be around 8.85 meters below ground level (MBGL). This represents a decline of about 0.39 meters from the previous year. The situation is particularly severe in some districts such as Chennai, Coimbatore, Erode, Karur, Krishnagiri, and Namakkal, where the groundwater level has been reported to be below 10 MBGL.

Rajasthan

Groundwater level status in Rajasthan : Critical

As of 2021, the CGWB reported that the groundwater level in most of the districts in Rajasthan was in the range of 10 to 30 meters (33 to 98 feet) below ground level (BGL). Some of the districts with the most severe groundwater depletion are Barmer, Jaisalmer, Jodhpur, and Nagaur, where the groundwater level is more than 30 meters (98 feet) below ground level.

Karnataka

Groundwater level status in Karnataka : Critical

In 2019, ground water level for Karnataka was 4.64 meters below ground level. Ground water level of Karnataka fell gradually from 9.96 meters below ground level in 2015 to 4.64 meters below ground level in 2019.

Andhra Pradesh

Groundwater level status in Andhra Pradesh : Declining

In 2020, ground water level for Andhra Pradesh was 14.83 meters below ground level. Though Andhra Pradesh ground water level fluctuated substantially in recent years, it tended to increase through 2014 - 2020 period ending at 14.83 meters below ground level in 2020.

Assam

Groundwater level status in Assam : Stable

During the pre-monsoon season (March 2021), the average groundwater level in Assam ranged from 0.23 meters below ground level (mbgl) in Dhemaji district to 23.83 mbgl in Jorhat district. During the post-monsoon season (January 2021), the average groundwater level ranged from 0.02 mbgl in Dhemaji district to 20.24 mbgl in Sivasagar district.

Chhattisgarh

Groundwater level status in Chhattisgarh : Critical

In 2019, ground water level for Chhattisgarh was 3.37 meters below ground level. Ground water level of Chhattisgarh fell gradually from 8.18 meters below ground level in 2015 to 3.37 meters below ground level in 2019.

Goa

Groundwater level status in Goa : Stable

In 2019, ground water level for Goa was 4.14 meters below ground level. Though Goa ground water level fluctuated substantially in recent years, it tended to decrease through 2015 - 2019 period ending at 4.14 meters below ground level in 2019.

Haryana

Groundwater level status in Haryana : Declining

Haryana ground water level was at level of 15.62 meters below ground level in 2019, down from 17.51 meters below ground level previous year, this is a change of 10.79%.

Himachal Pradesh

Groundwater level status in Himachal Pradesh : Critical

In 2019, ground water level for Himachal Pradesh was 5.2 meters below ground level. Ground water level of Himachal Pradesh fell gradually from 6.64 meters below ground level in 2015 to 5.2 meters below ground level in 2019

Jharkhand

Groundwater level status in Jharkhand : Critical

Chatra District is the top region by ground water level in India. As of 2019, ground water level in Chatra District was 6.37 meters below ground level that accounts for 7.52% of India's ground water level. The top 5 regions (others are Palamu, Kodarma District, Latehar, and Hazaribagh) account for 30.74% of it. India's total ground water level was estimated at 84.62 meters below ground level in 2019.

Kerala

Groundwater level status in Kerala : Declining

In 2021, ground water level for Kerala was 4.7 meters below ground level. Though Kerala ground water level fluctuated substantially in recent years, it tended to decrease through 2015 - 2021 period ending at 4.7 meters below ground level in 2021.

Delhi

Groundwater level status in Delhi : Critical

As of September 2021, the groundwater level in Delhi was estimated to be between 10 to 30 meters below ground level (mbgl) in most parts of the city, with some areas having even lower levels. In feet, this would roughly translate to a range between 33 to 98 feet below ground level.

Uttarakhand

Groundwater level status in Uttarkhand: Stable

As of 2019, ground water level in Almora District was 29.93 meters below ground level that accounts for 13.16% of India's ground water level. The top 5 regions (others are Bageshwar District, Chamoli District, Nainital District, and Garhwal) account for 57.06% of it. India's total ground water level was estimated at 227.45 meters below ground level in 2019.

Uttar Pradesh

Groundwater level status in Uttar Pradesh : Critical

In 2021, ground water level for Uttar Pradesh was 19.22 meters below ground level. Though Uttar Pradesh ground water level fluctuated substantially in recent years, it tended to increase through 2015 - 2021 period ending at 19.22 meters below ground level in 2021.
